# Case Study: Phishing Attack Targeting College Students

## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which an attacker impersonates a trusted entity — such as a bank, government authority, university, or popular service — to trick victims into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or financial details. It is one of the most widespread cybercrimes globally and increasingly targets students in India.

---

## Realistic Scenario

Riya, a first-year engineering student in Bhopal, received an email appearing to come from her university's IT department. The subject line read: *"Urgent: Verify Your Student Portal Account Within 24 Hours or Lose Access."*

The email contained the university logo, official-looking fonts, and a blue button labelled "Verify Now." Worried about losing access to her exam results, Riya clicked the link. It took her to a page that looked exactly like the VIT student portal. She entered her username, password, and then her Gmail credentials when prompted.

Within two hours, her email account was compromised. The attacker used her email to reset passwords on her LinkedIn and Kaggle accounts, accessed her saved documents, and attempted to log into her bank-linked app using her personal details found in her emails.

---

## How the Attack Happened (Step by Step)

1. Attacker registered a lookalike domain (e.g., *viit-bhopal.net* instead of *vitbhopal.ac.in*)
2. Sent a mass phishing email with urgency tactics ("24-hour deadline")
3. Victim clicked the link and landed on a cloned login page
4. Victim entered credentials, which were silently captured
5. Attacker used stolen credentials for account takeover and further fraud

---

## Who is Targeted?

First-year college students are especially vulnerable because they are new to managing their own digital accounts, unaware of institutional email patterns, and tend to act quickly under pressure.

---

## Consequences

- Loss of control over personal and academic accounts
- Potential financial fraud if banking apps are linked
- Emotional distress and privacy violation
- Difficulty recovering accounts without 2FA backup codes
